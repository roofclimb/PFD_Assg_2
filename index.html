OCBC Digital App

 

Question: I forgot my OCBC digital banking app PIN. How can I reset it?

Answer: Open the OCBC app and tap on 'Forgot PIN?'. This option is available on the PIN entry screen. You will need to answer some security questions to verify your identity. After answering correctly, you can create and confirm a new 6-digit PIN for the app.

Question: How do I transfer funds to another bank account using the OCBC app?

Answer: Tap on 'Transfers' in the OCBC app menu. Select 'Local Transfer to Other Banks'. Choose your transfer method (PayNow, FAST). Enter the recipient's bank and account number. Input the transfer amount and confirmation details when prompted. Review and confirm the details before authorizing the transfer.

Question: Can I pay bills using the OCBC mobile banking app?

Answer: Yes, the OCBC app allows you to pay bills easily. Go to 'Payments & Transfers' and select 'Pay Bills'. Choose the billing organization from the list. Enter your account number with them. Input the amount to pay and make the payment.

Question: How do I deposit a cheque using the OCBC mobile app?

Answer: Tap on 'Deposit' in the app menu. Select 'Deposit Cheque'. Endorse the physical cheque by signing on the back. Capture cheque images via the app by following the on-screen guide. Input the cheque details like amount, account number etc. Submit the images and details to complete your cheque deposit.

Online Banking

Question: I forgot my online banking password. What should I do?

Answer: Go to the login page and click on 'Forgot Password'. Enter your username and NRIC/Passport number when prompted. You will receive a one-time password (OTP) via SMS to your registered mobile number. Input this OTP and create a new password to reset your online banking password.

Question: How do I register for PayNow on OCBC Online Banking?

Answer: Log in to OCBC Online Banking and go to 'Payments/Transfers' > 'Manage PayNow'. Click on 'Register for PayNow'. Enter your NRIC/FIN number and mobile number. An SMS OTP will be sent to your mobile for verification. Once verified, you can start using PayNow to transfer funds instantly.

Question: What is the maximum transfer limit via PayNow per day?

Answer: You can transfer up to S$10,000 per day via PayNow using OCBC Online Banking. There is no limit to the number of PayNow transfers you can make within this S$10,000 daily limit.

Question: My debit card was retained by an OCBC ATM. What should I do?

Answer: Call the OCBC Helpline at 1800 363 3333 immediately and inform them about the retained card. Do not leave the ATM area until you have cancelled your card for security reasons. Visit the nearest OCBC branch to apply for a replacement debit card. Bring your NRIC/Passport for verification.

Question: How can I update my email address for OCBC Online Banking?

Answer: Log in to OCBC Online Banking and go to 'My Profile'. Under 'Contact Information', click on 'Update Email Address'. Enter your new email and click 'Send Verification Code'. Check your email for the verification code and input it to confirm and save your new email address.

ATM

 

Question: What is the QR Cash Withdrawal service about?

Answer: QR Cash Withdrawal is a fuss-free service that lets OCBC customers withdraw cash conveniently and swiftly without an ATM/Credit/Debit Card and PIN at any OCBC ATM, whether it is the normal OCBC ATM, ATM Plus or new ATMNEW. Simply scan the QR code on the ATM screen with the OCBC Digital app to withdraw cash. No card is required. What's more, you can minimise contact with the ATM.

Question: Who can use the QR Cash Withdrawal service?

Answer: The service is available to all OCBC personal banking customers who have OCBC Digital Banking access with eligible bank accounts linked to it. Please also ensure you have OCBC Digital app installed in your mobile phone.

Question: How do I make a QR cash withdrawal?

Answer: At any ATM, ATM Plus or the new ATM: Tap on “Withdraw cash with OCBC Digital app” button at the bottom left corner of the ATM screen. A QR code will be displayed on the ATM screen. Scan the QR code using your OCBC Digital app. Enter the withdrawal amount, select the account to debit from, then tap on Confirm. Collect your cash from the ATM! It's that easy!

Question: What is the withdrawal limit for QR Cash Withdrawal service?

Answer: The daily limit for QR cash withdrawal is set at S$1,000.

Question: I have exceeded my ATM card withdrawal/PayNow limit. Can I still perform a QR cash withdrawal?

Yes, you can still perform a QR cash withdrawal as it has a separate limit from your ATM card withdrawal/PayNow limit.

Question: What should I do if I change my mind and decided not to proceed with the QR cash withdrawal transaction?

Answer: At the normal OCBC ATM/ATM Plus, you can press the cancel button on the ATM keypad and logout from the OCBC Digital app to discontinue with the cash withdrawal transaction.

At the OCBC New ATM, you may tap on the exit button at the top right corner of the ATM screen and logout from the OCBC Digital app to discontinue with the cash withdrawal transaction

Card Security

Question: My OCBC credit/debit card was stolen. What should I do?

Answer: Call the 24-hour OCBC Helpline immediately at 1800 363 3333 to report your card as lost/stolen. Provide your name, card number, expiry date and any other details requested. This will allow OCBC to block the card and prevent unauthorized transactions. Visit any OCBC branch to apply for a replacement card on the same day.

Question: I found some suspicious charges on my OCBC card statement. How do I dispute them?

Answer: Log in to OCBC Online Banking and go to Account Services > Dispute Transactions. Select the transaction(s) to dispute and submit your dispute request by providing details on why you believe it is unauthorized. Alternatively, you can file a dispute by calling the OCBC hotline or visiting any branch.

Question: My OCBC credit card details were used for online fraud. What should I do?

Answer: Immediately call the OCBC Helpline to report the online fraud incident and cancel your card. Provide details on the fraudulent transactions detected. Visit an OCBC branch to apply for a replacement card with new card number and CVV code. Also change your online banking passwords as a precaution.

Question: How can I prevent phishing scams targeting my OCBC account?

Answer: OCBC will never ask for your passwords, OTPs, CVV or account details via calls, SMS or emails. Verify any suspicious requests by contacting OCBC directly through official channels only. Do not click links or provide details to third-parties. Keep software updated and use strong anti-virus protection.

Question: What is the card activation process for newly issued OCBC cards?

Answer: For new OCBC cards, activate them by calling the number on the activation sticker on the card. Alternatively, activate via Online Banking by going to Card Services > Activate New Card and entering card details. Lastly, you can activate in-person at any OCBC ATM or branch.
